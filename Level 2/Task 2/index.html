Subhash Chandra Bose

1897-1945


Subhash Chandra Bose, also known as Netaji, was a prominent Indian freedom fighter and leader of the Indian National Congress during the Indian independence movement against British rule.

He was born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, and studied philosophy at the University of Calcutta. He later went to England to study for the Indian Civil Service examination, but resigned from his position in 1921 to join the Indian independence movement.

Achievements

  • Formed the Indian National Army (INA) to fight against the British during World War II.
  • President of Indian National Congress twice.
  • Made significant contributions in the Indian independence movement.

Timeline

  • 1897: Born in Cuttack, Odisha.
  • 1921: Resigned from Indian Civil Service to join the Indian independence movement.
  • 1942: Formed the Indian National Army (INA).
  • 1945: Disappeared on 18th August, presumed dead in a plane crash.

Subhash Chandra Bose's bravery and sacrifice will always be remembered in the fight for India's freedom.
